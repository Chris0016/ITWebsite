What are Integrated Circuits?
Christopher PerezJune 01, 2021 at 5:34 pm

Integrated circuits and Chips

Behind all the intricacies of a computer lay many integrated circuits that handle processing and some memory functions. Integrated Circuits come in all shapes and sizes, and serve different purposes.

Circuits can be made using discrete electronic components like resistors(link), transistors(link) and capacitors(link). However, the integration these devices creates an integrated circuit, hence the name 'Integrated Circuit'. Inside of a Intergrated circuit there are resistors, capacitors and so on, but these devices are made to fit a very small chip.A very important technology involved int he process of making integrated circuits is photoligraphy.

Chips, which are combinations of integrated circuits, are those black small rectangles that are found on the motherboard. Each of these chips on the motherboard have millions of other microscopic parts that work together to give high-speed processing.

History

In the modern world, chips can be found on just about every digital devices, and these ensure that devices can be small but also very powerful, but it always wasn't that way. Before chips and ICs digital computers were made of thousands of discrete electronic parts. These electronic devices were made to control and direct the flow of electrons.

ENIAC computer picture You may have seen(in pictures) those large room-sized computers that seemed like an art project gone wrong. Although practically none-existent now, these giant computers were the first making of what a digital computer was like even though could only do simple operations.

The first Integrated Circuit was developed in 1958 by Jack Kilby. Months after Kilby's creation, the first 'pure' or monolithic integrated circuit created by Robert Noyce. The IC developed by Kilby was a hybrid,meaning that it was not completely digital, and was not viable for mass production in contrast to the Integrated Circuit developed by Noyce.

Types of Integrated Circuits

Integrated Circuits can roughly be partitioned into three types; Analog, Digital, and Hybrid.

Analog ICs use an array of different voltages and currents along its circuitry. By contrast digital ICs, use voltage and current at two distinct levels that are interpreted as ones and zeroes. Because they tend to be a bit bulkier than digital ICs couple with the fact that most devices are digital, Analog ICs have been almost completely replaced by digital circuits. There is still some spaces were analog ICs fit perfectly, like voltage regulators. For instance, because power can be supplied to a computer at different voltages, the power supply voltage chip tends to have an analog IC.

Digital Integrated Circuits

Digital ICs, as mentioned before, use voltage and current at two discrete levels. Furthermore, digital ICs are smaller than analog ICs and are suitable for a broad range of applications.

Digital ICs can be broken down into more categories such as logic, memory, interface, and power management, and programable devices. This article will go over some of the more common types.

Logic ICs

Logic gates are essential to executing programs and commands. However, like other parts, making logic gates out of discrete parts is impractical. Logic ICs are made of smaller logic gate ICs that represent conditions like 'AND', 'OR', 'XOR', and 'NAND'. Logic gate ICs are used in applications that need processing or control structures like the microcontroller.

Memory ICs

MOS memory Floating point gate MOSFET(FGMOS)

The importance of memory in any computer cannot be overstated. In desktops and laptops alike, RAM holds the operating system along with other cache used by the user. The technology behind this is Floating Point Gate MOSFET(FGMOS) transistors.

You can read more about FGMOS here.

Power Management ICs

As mentioned before , analog ICs are often used in power management, but this does not exclude from digital ICs from having the capabilities to act as a power manager. The power management category encompasses many topics. power management include Battery management ICs, Dc/DC switching regulators, DDR memory power ICs, Date drivers, and more.

Programable Devices

A programmable logic device(PLD) is an electronic component used to build programmable devices. Unlike ordinary ICs which have a specified function, programable logic devices are undefined at the time of manufacture. PLD are programmed using a specialized programs called hardware description languages(HDLs).
